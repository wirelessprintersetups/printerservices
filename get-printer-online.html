Get Printer Back Online

  1. Home
  2. Get Printer Back Online

When your printer suddenly shows up as “offline,” it can bring your workday to a standstill. Whether you’re in the middle of printing an office document or just trying to get your boarding pass ready, an offline printer can be frustrating. The good news is that getting your printer back online is often easier than you think. In this guide, we’ll walk through quick fixes first, then dive into detailed troubleshooting and preventative measures.

Why Printers Go Offline

Before jumping into solutions, it helps to know why this happens. Most of the time, printers go offline due to:

  • • Connection problems: Loose USB cables or unstable WiFi.
  • • Driver issues: Outdated or corrupted drivers can break communication.
  • • Wrong default settings: If your computer is trying to print to the wrong device.
  • • Queue errors: Too many stuck print jobs.
  • • Network policies or firewalls: Especially common in office setups.

Knowing these causes will make troubleshooting smoother.

Quick Fix: Restart and Reconnect

The fastest way to get a printer back online is also the simplest: restart everything.

  • 1. Turn off your printer and unplug it for 30 seconds.
  • 2. Restart your computer or mobile device.
  • 3. If using WiFi, reboot your router.

Step 1: Check Physical Connections

For wired printers, ensure the USB cable is securely connected to both the printer and the computer. Try a different port if necessary. For network printers, check that the Ethernet cable is plugged in correctly or that the WiFi indicator light is stable.

Step 2: Set the Printer as Default

Your printer may show as “offline” if the system is trying to send jobs to another device.

Windows: Open Control Panel > Devices and Printers, right-click your printer, and select Set as Default.

Mac: Go to System Settings > Printers & Scanners and choose your printer as default.

This ensures your print jobs are sent to the correct machine.

Step 3: Disable “Use Printer Offline” Mode

On Windows devices, there’s a setting that can force the printer offline.

  • 1. Go to Devices and Printers.
  • 2. Right-click your printer and select See What’s Printing.
  • 3. In the new window, click Printer on the menu bar.
  • 4. Make sure Use Printer Offline is unchecked.

This quick change often resolves the issue.

Step 4: Update or Reinstall Drivers

Printer drivers are the software that lets your computer talk to your printer. If they are outdated or corrupted, your printer may stay offline.

  • • Visit the manufacturer’s website (HP, Canon, Epson, etc.).
  • • Download the latest driver for your exact printer model.
  • • Install the update and restart your computer.

On Mac, you may also need to reset the printing system and add the printer again.

Step 5: Clear Print Queue

Sometimes the problem lies not with the printer, but with the queue. A stuck document can keep the printer in an offline state.

  • • Windows: Open the queue window (Devices and Printers > See What’s Printing). Right-click and Cancel All Documents.
  • • Mac: Go to Printers & Scanners, select your printer, and clear the pending jobs.

Once cleared, try printing a test page.

Step 6: Reconnect Wireless Printers

If you’re using a wireless printer, WiFi connectivity issues are often the culprit.

  • • Use the printer’s display panel to reconnect to your home or office WiFi network.
  • • Enter your password carefully, ensuring no typos.
  • • If your router has both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands, try connecting the printer to the 2.4 GHz band, since many printers don’t support 5 GHz.

For routers with a WPS button, you can press it and connect directly without typing the password.

Advanced Troubleshooting

If the basics don’t work, try these advanced tips:

  • • Assign a static IP address to your printer so it doesn’t change every time your router restarts.
  • • Check firewall or antivirus settings that may be blocking the connection.
  • • Reset the printer to factory settings and reconfigure it from scratch.

    • For office users, it may also be necessary to ask IT to re-add the printer via IP address or network name.

Preventing Future Offline Issues

Once you’ve brought your printer back online, take steps to avoid repeats:

  • • Keep drivers updated by enabling automatic updates.
  • • Place your WiFi printer near the router to reduce signal drops.
  • • Regularly restart your printer and router.
  • • Use quality cables if you’re connecting via USB.

Final Thoughts

Printers going offline is a universal frustration, but with the right steps, it’s also one of the easiest problems to fix. Start with restarts and connection checks, then move on to default settings and driver updates. For WiFi printers, a quick reconnection often does the trick.

By understanding why printers go offline and applying both quick fixes and preventative measures, you’ll spend less time troubleshooting and more time getting your documents printed.
